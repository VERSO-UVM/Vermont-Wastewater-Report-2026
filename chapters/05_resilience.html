Environment Disaster and Wastewater Resilience#

This chapter assesses the resilience of Vermont’s wastewater systems to environmental disasters and climate change impacts.

Introduction#

Climate change and extreme weather events pose significant risks to wastewater infrastructure. This chapter examines:

  • Vulnerability assessment of wastewater facilities

  • Historical impacts from flooding, storms, and extreme weather

  • Climate change projections and implications

  • Resilience strategies and adaptation measures

Climate Change Impacts on Wastewater Systems#

Key Climate Hazards#

  1. Flooding - River and flash flooding

  2. Extreme Precipitation - Increased stormwater infiltration

  3. Temperature Changes - Effects on treatment processes

  4. Drought - Impacts on dilution and receiving water quality

Resilience Assessment Framework#

Evaluation criteria for assessing wastewater system resilience:

  1. Physical Resilience - Infrastructure hardening and elevation

  2. Operational Resilience - Emergency procedures and backup systems

  3. Financial Resilience - Insurance and emergency funding

  4. Institutional Resilience - Planning and coordination
